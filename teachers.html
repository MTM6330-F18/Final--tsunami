About me

What courses do you teach?

I teach Client-Side Web Development and Server-Side Web Development for the IMD program.

Where did you go to school university, college, etc.)?

I attended Iowa State University

Why did you become a teacher?

I am largely self-taught and constantly striving to learn more. The field of web design and development is ever-changing and if you don’t keep moving, you will fall behind. It is this realization that lead me to start teaching at Algonquin College in January of 2016. I have a passion for learning and it fuels my desire to share what I know with others. It has become my new passion, and my primary focus. I aim to be the best teacher I can, and it is my hope that my students will find the same spark of curiosity that changed my life all those years ago.

How many years have you been a teacher?

I have been teaching for almost 3 years.

Is there anything you wish you taught but can't?

There are a lot of topics and information I wish I could teach. Web Development is a vast field and there is so much that can be learned and taught. But unfortunately, there is only so much time. But specifically, I would like to teach more CSS. I really enjoy how CSS mixes the worlds of logic and design.

Advice for future students:

Don’t be afraid of new things and embrace change. In this field, things are changing all the time, and there is a constant array of new things to learn. You can choose to sit by the sidelines or you can be the one who is riding the wave.

MAD9124 Mobile API Development

Where did you go to school university, college, etc.)?

I have studied at Carleton, Laurier and the school of hard knocks ;-)

Why did you become a teacher?

My professional career, has always included coaching and mentoring components. When the opportunity came up to teach at Algonquin this seemed like a natural extension. I really enjoy the opportunity to share my knowledge and passion for our industry with the next generation of web design and development professionals.

How many years have you been a teacher?

I have been formally teaching at Algonquin for two years. I have been coach colleagues over my whole 30 year career.

Is there anything you wish you taught but can't?

I would love to add a special course dedicated to implementing web accessibility best practices. I think this is an important and often overlooked topic in our industry.

Advice for future students:

Come with an open mind. Don’t be afraid of what you don’t know -- yet. If you are a little uncomfortable, that means that you are learning something new and that is a good thing. We can guide you through the learning process, and if you are willing to do the reading and the practice you can learn to do anything!
